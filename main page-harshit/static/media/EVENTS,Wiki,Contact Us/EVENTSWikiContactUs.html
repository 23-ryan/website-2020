Wondering what we do?

From helping people build their first Remote Controlled Car to assisting them in acing their Arduino programming skills, we conduct various exciting events for the tech enthusiastic community of IIT Bombay. We also hold sessions to help you learn about things as simple as Basic Electronics to topics as advanced as Microcontrollers! Here is a glimpse of some of our events-

1)XLR8

The first Technical Competition of the year and also our top-notch flagship event - XLR8!

3 Weeks                                  600+ Students                                    A race to the finish line!

You are now a member of a team of four. Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to design a remote-controlled bot from scratch. Using your phone, you should be able to manoeuvre the bot safely through a track filled with a diverse set of obstacles. Be warned; The clock is ticking!

The team which completes the task in the minimum amount of time wins. There are also prizes for other aspects of the competition, which include design and creativity aspects. So, put your thinking caps on and start getting your hands dirty!

2)Get Electrified Sessions

Ever wondered how a sound amplifier works? Or what a DJ actually does by turning some knobs? Can we solve differential equations using hardware? What is digital logic design?

To answer these and many more of your curious questions, Electronics and Robotics Club conducts several Get Electrified sessions throughout the year.  These are really informative & fun hands-on sessions where we will give you an introduction to analog and digital electronics. In the analog session, we will implement circuits and see actual waveforms. We shall also guide you on the usage of standard lab equipment like a power supply, DSO and a function generator. In the Digital electronics session, we will make you comfortable with various circuits such as adders, MUX, DeMUX, PRIORITY encoders, decoders and learn their amazing applications. Finally, using all the things discussed, you shall be building a cool project!

Be sure not to miss these sessions!

3)Introduction to Arduino

   Do you know Ar-do-we-know?😛

 Well, if not, don’t worry. The Electronics and Robotics Club has got your back! We organise the Arduino Bootcamp to get you started with programming in Arduino.

Have no prior programming experience? No worries again, we’ll start from scratch and show you the power of Arduino with hands-on experience in the session. Believe us, with this kickstart, and some practise of your own, you’ll be creating your own Arduino projects in no time!

4)Line Follower

Remember that awesome bot you made during XLR8? Now it's time to teach your bot to think and make its own decisions!

Now you have a modified mission- build an autonomous robot that can detect and follow a white path on a black backdrop-using the Arduino microcontroller! This will not only introduce you to microcontroller programming, but you will also get a flavour of control systems while implementing a PID algorithm. Note that there will be sharp turns, junctions, gaps in the track, and some surprise elements in the contest to make it more fun.

Important notice:

  • You can modify your existing XLR8 bot or make a new one from scratch.
  • Perks of being a part of Line follower - Top 10 teams will get direct entry to Meshmerize Techfest Finals ( Techfest competition for line follower bots with some modifications).

5)Blahs

Throughout the year the ERC also holds various informative ‘Blah’ sessions where our own seniors will share their knowledge and experience on a topic they have worked on. There are no prerequisites to attend these talks. As always, eagerness to learn is all that is required. Have a look at the Blahs conducted last year-

 

  • Optimisation for Digital Circuits

The First BLAH of the semester was based on Network Optimization for Digital Circuits. This also helped for the internship season as the speaker even discussed the overall experience of working at Texas Instruments along with the selection process.

About the speaker:

Sahasrajit Sarmasarkar, a fourth-year undergraduate in the Electrical Engineering Department, interned at Texas Instruments as a digital intern. His project was related to network optimisations for running a combinational digital circuit at higher speeds using wave-pipelining techniques and changing wire connections between cells without changing the overall functionality of the circuit. He also worked on a VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) problem to select cells from a pool of cells meeting the overall delay target with minimal cost. These problems were formulated as a linear programming problem, and standard solvers were used to solve it.

  • Augmented Reality

The talk covered the basics of Augmented Reality, - the present and future applications. It also focussed on how to get started into Computer Graphics and Augmented Reality. Some challenges and possible solutions to this technology were also discussed. Some state of art AR Headsets were even touched upon.

           About the speaker:

Kumar Ashutosh, a 4th-year Electrical Engineering undergraduate, has been working on Augmented Reality applications for a couple of years now. Some of his previous works include new concept AR Games, AR for city navigation and similar emerging applications.

  •  Control Theory & PID

Ever seen and wondered how robots interact with the world so very human-like and how "controlled" their actions are? How does a bot know how much force to apply to just lift a body off the ground, neither too fast nor too slow?

To answer these, ERC conducted a Blah on Control Theory and PID. From the simplest of systems like a line follower to sophisticated robotic arms and humanoids, this is the one key to all of them. The session covered all from the basics of control theory.

About the speakers:

 Dikshant Shehmar and Pavan Kale were the speakers who have done several projects in this field. We also had some MATLAB simulations to help people understand the controller more intuitively.

8) ROS workshops

Ever wondered how to simulate robots? How to coordinate between drones? How to program a robot to map its environment?

The answer to every question is ROS- Robot Operating System. Ubiquitous in Robotics, it is a robotic middleware providing software libraries and tools to build a robot application. With robots playing an increasingly crucial role in our lives, ROS is set to experience a revolutionary growth in the future.

ERC conducted a 5-week workshop for the novice on the same, which witnessed an overwhelming participation of 300+ students with 40+ students completing the course. The session was intended to strike a balance between understanding concepts and then implementing the same through tutorials and assignments given at the end of every week

The key takeaways include:

1.Understanding the ROS architecture (understand packages written by developers so that one can tweak and modify)

2. Programming and configuring basic robotic tasks such as communication between different nodes and navigating through obstacles autonomously.

3. Getting acquainted with ROS tools such as gazebo, a robotic simulator, and Rviz for 3D visualisation

4. Making your own 2-wheeled bot using URDF and giving it various plugins and properties.

5. Implementing G mapping for SLAM in gazebo simulator using DWA algorithm.

LINUX FOR ROBOTICS
